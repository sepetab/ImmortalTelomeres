For more information regarding this workshop, please read here.
12:00 - 12:30 Prof. J.J. Samuels Read Read codes preceded SNOMED and were ubiquitous throughout the United Kingdom up until 2018. These codes describe a patients situation, including their occupation, social circumstances, and ethnicity.
12:30 - 13:00 A.Prof. C. Barkley SNOMED SNOMED is a involves a collection of encoded medical terms, synonyms and definitions used for documentation and reporting. The primary purpose of this system is to support the clinical encodings of patient records to improve patient care. To elaborate, by employing the use of a common terminology for medical phenomena, the interpretation of patient information is made more robust.
13:00 - 14:00 Break
14:00 - 15:00 (Plenary Session) Messaging Standards Dr. K.L.P Carter HL7 Having its origins in 1987, HL7 was developed by an international community of healthcare experts. Today, HL7 has dedicated developers who are accredited by the American National Standards Institute. These developers provide a set of message formats that describe an ideal presentation of clinical information. To learn more, please visit here.
15:00 - 15:30 Dr. M.W. Sam FHIR FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) is a standard which describe data formats and APIs. FHIR was created by HL7 and employs a modern web-based suite of API technologies. The main goal of this messaging standard is to assist the interoperability between legacy health care systems.
15:30-16:00 Dr. A. Ally ISO/IEEE 11073 The ISO/IEEE 11073 is a standard which enables communication between medical and healthcare devices and computers. This standard outlines the method of automatic and detailed electronic data capture of patient medical data.
